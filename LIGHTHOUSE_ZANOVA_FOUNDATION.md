# LIGHTHOUSE CAMPUS — ZANOVA FOUNDATION

**Institutional Operating Model — v1.0**
**Classification:** Internal. Not published on the public website.
**Purpose:** Contractual bridge between the Lighthouse Campus public
institution and the future **ZANOVA Institutional Intelligence Platform**.
**Doctrine:** Aligned with the IN-BiiS Civilizational Constitution v1.0 —
Institutional Intelligence Engineering / *هندسة الذكاء المؤسسي*.
**Companion documents:** `LIGHTHOUSE_WEBSITE_ARCHITECTURE.md`,
`LIGHTHOUSE_ZANOVA_MAPPING.md`, `LIGHTHOUSE_CAMPUS_WEBSITE_CONTENT_v1.md`.

---

## 0. Framing

The Lighthouse Campus website presents the public institution. This
document extracts the institution *behind* that surface: its domains,
capabilities, actors, journeys, information objects, decisions and
intelligence opportunities. It is the ZANOVA specification seed.

Guiding principle (Art. VI, IN-BiiS Constitution): **execution is the
system that binds all stages**. Every capability below is described so
that a future ZANOVA module can render its execution state visible,
governable and accountable.

## PART 2 — BUSINESS CAPABILITY MAP

Each capability is described in the shape ZANOVA will consume:
*purpose · inputs · outputs · owner · stakeholders · dependencies ·
digital support · future ZANOVA module*.

### 2.1 Leadership
- **Purpose:** Set direction; make and communicate consequential decisions.
- **Inputs:** Institutional intelligence briefings, governance decisions, community signals.
- **Outputs:** Strategic decisions, priorities, public statements.
- **Owner:** Head of Campus.
- **Stakeholders:** Board, staff, families, community.
- **Dependencies:** Governance, all operational domains.
- **Digital Support:** Leadership Workspace, Executive Dashboard.
- **ZANOVA Module:** `Leadership & Executive Intelligence`.

### 2.2 Governance
- **Purpose:** Ensure the institution operates per its founding purpose (Art. X).
- **Inputs:** Policies, performance data, audits, risk signals.
- **Outputs:** Approvals, policy revisions, accountability reports.
- **Owner:** Board / Governance Body.
- **Stakeholders:** Leadership, families, community, regulators.
- **Dependencies:** All domains (as oversight).
- **Digital Support:** Governance Workspace, policy registry, risk register.
- **ZANOVA Module:** `Governance & Accountability`.

### 2.3 Academic Affairs / Curriculum
- **Purpose:** Design and maintain a coherent, ambitious programme.
- **Inputs:** Standards, learner data, teacher feedback, research.
- **Outputs:** Curriculum, subject plans, learning objectives.
- **Owner:** Academic Leader.
- **Stakeholders:** Teachers, learners, families.
- **Dependencies:** Teaching & Learning, Assessment, Learning Journey.
- **Digital Support:** Curriculum registry, subject catalogue.
- **ZANOVA Module:** `Academic Model`.

### 2.4 Teaching & Learning
- **Purpose:** Deliver pedagogy grounded in mentorship and expertise.
- **Inputs:** Curriculum, learner profiles, teaching framework.
- **Outputs:** Lessons, learning experiences, feedback.
- **Owner:** Academic Leader + Teachers.
- **Stakeholders:** Learners, families.
- **Dependencies:** Curriculum, Assessment, Student Development.
- **Digital Support:** Teacher Workspace, planning tools.
- **ZANOVA Module:** `Teaching Practice`.

### 2.5 Assessment
- **Purpose:** Support learning through evidence and feedback.
- **Inputs:** Learning objectives, learner work, teacher judgement.
- **Outputs:** Feedback, records, reports, promotion decisions.
- **Owner:** Academic Leader.
- **Stakeholders:** Teachers, learners, families.
- **Dependencies:** Curriculum, Teaching & Learning.
- **Digital Support:** Assessment records, reporting.
- **ZANOVA Module:** `Assessment & Feedback`.

### 2.6 Learning Journey
- **Purpose:** Design the continuous path from Early Years to graduation.
- **Inputs:** Stage frameworks, learner profile, graduate profile.
- **Outputs:** Stage transitions, pathway decisions.
- **Owner:** Academic Leader + Stage Leads.
- **Stakeholders:** Teachers, learners, families.
- **Dependencies:** Curriculum, Assessment, Student Development.
- **Digital Support:** Journey visualisation per learner.
- **ZANOVA Module:** `Learning Journey`.

### 2.7 Student Development & Wellbeing
- **Purpose:** Care for the whole learner — character, confidence, health.
- **Inputs:** Learner signals, family input, wellbeing framework.
- **Outputs:** Support plans, referrals, mentoring notes.
- **Owner:** Wellbeing Lead.
- **Stakeholders:** Teachers, families, health services.
- **Dependencies:** Health, Teaching & Learning, Parent Partnership.
- **Digital Support:** Wellbeing Workspace (private).
- **ZANOVA Module:** `Wellbeing`.

### 2.8 Student Affairs (Clubs, Athletics, Arts, Service)
- **Purpose:** Life beyond the timetable — belonging and contribution.
- **Inputs:** Learner interests, activity offer.
- **Outputs:** Rosters, events, recognition.
- **Owner:** Student Life Lead.
- **Stakeholders:** Learners, families, coaches, mentors.
- **Dependencies:** Events, Campus Operations.
- **Digital Support:** Activities catalogue, sign-up.
- **ZANOVA Module:** `Activities & Student Life`.

### 2.9 Admissions
- **Purpose:** Considered, welcoming entry to the community.
- **Inputs:** Enquiries, applications, visits, references.
- **Outputs:** Offers, cohorts, family onboarding.
- **Owner:** Admissions Officer.
- **Stakeholders:** Applicants, families, Academic Office, Finance.
- **Dependencies:** Academic Affairs, Finance, Communications.
- **Digital Support:** Admissions Workspace + applicant view.
- **ZANOVA Module:** `Admissions`.

### 2.10 Parent Partnership
- **Purpose:** Families as educational partners.
- **Inputs:** Communications, journey milestones, learner data.
- **Outputs:** Meetings, updates, agreements.
- **Owner:** Family Relations Lead.
- **Stakeholders:** Families, Teachers, Leadership.
- **Dependencies:** All learner-touching domains.
- **Digital Support:** Parent Portal.
- **ZANOVA Module:** `Family Partnership`.

### 2.11 Community Engagement

## PART 3 — INSTITUTIONAL ACTORS

For each actor: *responsibilities · interactions · permissions ·
information required · information produced*.

### 3.1 Learner (Student)
- **Responsibilities:** Engage in learning, contribute to community, care for self and others.
- **Interactions:** Teachers, mentors, peers, family, wellbeing staff.
- **Permissions:** Read own record; contribute to portfolio; join activities.
- **Information Required:** Timetable, assignments, feedback, activities, calendar.
- **Information Produced:** Coursework, reflections, activity records.

### 3.2 Parent / Guardian (Family)
- **Responsibilities:** Partner with the school in the learner's growth.
- **Interactions:** Homeroom teacher, wellbeing lead, admissions, finance.
- **Permissions:** Read child's record; communicate with staff; manage finance.
- **Information Required:** Progress, wellbeing, calendar, fees, policies.
- **Information Produced:** Consent, feedback, meeting notes.

### 3.3 Teacher
- **Responsibilities:** Design and deliver learning; mentor learners; communicate with families.
- **Interactions:** Learners, families, colleagues, academic leadership.
- **Permissions:** Read/write class and learner records within scope.
- **Information Required:** Curriculum, learner profiles, assessment history.
- **Information Produced:** Plans, feedback, assessments, reports.

### 3.4 Mentor
- **Responsibilities:** Guide learners in character, direction and choices.
- **Interactions:** Learners, families, teachers, wellbeing.
- **Permissions:** Read learner profile; write mentoring notes.
- **Information Required:** Learner journey, wellbeing signals.
- **Information Produced:** Mentoring records, referrals.

### 3.5 Principal / Head of Campus
- **Responsibilities:** Institutional direction and daily leadership.
- **Interactions:** Board, staff, families, community, regulators.
- **Permissions:** Full institutional read; approve strategic actions.
- **Information Required:** Institutional intelligence briefings.
- **Information Produced:** Decisions, directives, public statements.

### 3.6 Academic Leader
- **Responsibilities:** Curriculum, teaching practice, assessment integrity.
- **Interactions:** Teachers, Principal, families (on academic matters).
- **Permissions:** Academic scope: curriculum, assessment, teacher development.
- **Information Required:** Learner performance, teacher performance.
- **Information Produced:** Curriculum changes, development plans.

### 3.7 Admissions Officer
- **Responsibilities:** Manage the admissions journey.
- **Interactions:** Applicants, families, academic office, finance.
- **Permissions:** Manage applications, offers, cohorts.
- **Information Required:** Applications, capacity, policy.
- **Information Produced:** Offers, admissions decisions, cohort lists.

### 3.8 Finance Officer
- **Responsibilities:** Fees, invoices, scholarships, budgets.
- **Interactions:** Families, HR, Leadership.
- **Permissions:** Finance module full access.
- **Information Required:** Enrolments, fee schedules, budgets.
- **Information Produced:** Invoices, payments, financial reports.

### 3.9 HR Officer
- **Responsibilities:** Recruitment, contracts, staff development.
- **Interactions:** Applicants, staff, Leadership.
- **Permissions:** HR module full access.
- **Information Required:** Job needs, applications, contracts.
- **Information Produced:** Hires, records, development plans.

### 3.10 Nurse / Health Services
- **Responsibilities:** Health care on campus; medical records.
- **Interactions:** Learners, families, wellbeing, staff.
- **Permissions:** Medical records under strict access.
- **Information Required:** Medical history, consents.
- **Information Produced:** Health records, incident logs.

### 3.11 Librarian
- **Responsibilities:** Curate the library; support reading and study.
- **Interactions:** Learners, teachers, families.
- **Permissions:** Library catalogue and circulation.
- **Information Required:** Catalogue, curriculum, learner interests.
- **Information Produced:** Circulation records, reading programmes.

### 3.12 Laboratory Technician
- **Responsibilities:** Prepare and safeguard laboratory activity.
- **Interactions:** Science teachers, learners.
- **Permissions:** Lab inventory, safety records.
- **Information Required:** Lesson plans, safety protocols.
- **Information Produced:** Inventory, incident logs.

### 3.13 Driver / Transport
- **Responsibilities:** Safe transport to and from campus.
- **Interactions:** Families, learners, safety.
- **Permissions:** Route and passenger data (scoped).
- **Information Required:** Routes, rosters, safety training.
- **Information Produced:** Trip logs, incident reports.

### 3.14 Visitor
- **Responsibilities:** Observe campus protocols.
- **Interactions:** Reception, host.
- **Permissions:** Escorted access only.
- **Information Required:** Visit purpose, host.
- **Information Produced:** Visitor log entry.

### 3.15 Applicant
- **Responsibilities:** Complete the application journey honestly.
- **Interactions:** Admissions, family.
- **Permissions:** Manage own application.
- **Information Required:** Requirements, timelines.
- **Information Produced:** Application, documents, interview outcomes.

### 3.16 Alumnus
- **Responsibilities:** Represent Lighthouse Campus in the world.
- **Interactions:** Community office, current learners (mentoring).
- **Permissions:** Alumni network.
- **Information Required:** Alumni events, opportunities.
- **Information Produced:** Stories, mentorship contributions.

### 3.17 Community Partner
- **Responsibilities:** Deliver on a values-aligned partnership.
- **Interactions:** Community office, Leadership.
- **Permissions:** Partnership workspace access.
- **Information Required:** Partnership goals, calendar.
- **Information Produced:** Partnership deliverables, reports.

## PART 4 — JOURNEY MAPS

For each journey: *trigger · steps · decisions · outputs · automation
opportunities*. Automation follows Art. VI: make execution visible,
never automate judgement.

### 4.1 Admissions Journey
- **Trigger:** Family enquiry or visit request.
- **Steps:** Enquiry → Visit → Application → Assessment → Interview → Offer → Acceptance → Onboarding.
- **Decisions:** Fit, capacity, scholarship, cohort placement.
- **Outputs:** Enrolled learner and onboarded family.
- **Automation Opportunities:** Enquiry triage, document checklist, timeline nudges, cohort suggestions.

### 4.2 Learner Journey
- **Trigger:** Enrolment into an Early Years, Primary, Preparatory or Secondary cohort.
- **Steps:** Onboarding → Daily learning → Assessments → Stage transitions → Graduation Pathway.
- **Decisions:** Promotion, support, pathway.
- **Outputs:** Growth records, portfolio, graduation.
- **Automation Opportunities:** Progress dashboards, wellbeing signals, portfolio assembly.

### 4.3 Family Journey
- **Trigger:** Offer accepted.
- **Steps:** Onboarding → Ongoing communication → Meetings → Milestones → Graduation.
- **Decisions:** Consent, engagement level, escalation.
- **Outputs:** Sustained partnership.
- **Automation Opportunities:** Calendar sync, personalised digests, meeting scheduling.

### 4.4 Teacher Journey
- **Trigger:** Job posting → recruitment.
- **Steps:** Application → Interview → Onboarding → Teaching → Development → Reviews.
- **Decisions:** Hire, tenure, development, promotion.
- **Outputs:** Effective teachers, institutional knowledge.
- **Automation Opportunities:** Lesson-planning support, feedback aggregation, development pathways.

### 4.5 Recruitment Journey (HR)
- **Trigger:** Vacancy identified.
- **Steps:** Job specification → Posting → Screening → Interviews → Offer → Onboarding.
- **Decisions:** Shortlist, offer, contract.
- **Outputs:** New hires.
- **Automation Opportunities:** Screening, scheduling, reference tracking.

### 4.6 Employee Journey
- **Trigger:** Contract signed.
- **Steps:** Onboarding → Role → Development → Recognition → Exit / Transition.
- **Decisions:** Development plan, promotion, retention.
- **Outputs:** Sustained institutional capability.
- **Automation Opportunities:** Development records, competency tracking.

### 4.7 Visitor Journey
- **Trigger:** Visit scheduled.
- **Steps:** Registration → Arrival → Escort → Visit → Departure.
- **Decisions:** Access level.
- **Outputs:** Visit record.
- **Automation Opportunities:** Pre-registration, badges, host notifications.

### 4.8 Community Partnership Journey
- **Trigger:** Partnership proposal.
- **Steps:** Discovery → Alignment review → Agreement → Delivery → Review.
- **Decisions:** Alignment with values, approval.
- **Outputs:** Active partnership.
- **Automation Opportunities:** Alignment checklist, milestone tracking.

### 4.9 Graduation Journey
- **Trigger:** Entering the graduation year.
- **Steps:** Pathway selection → University guidance → Applications → Offers → Graduation → Alumni transition.
- **Decisions:** Pathway, university choice.
- **Outputs:** Graduate ready for university and life.
- **Automation Opportunities:** Pathway visualisation, deadline tracking, alumni onboarding.

## PART 6 — DECISION MODEL

Institutional decisions in ZANOVA are first-class objects. Each carries
*owner · inputs · policies · evidence · outputs*. Every decision
produces a `DecisionRecord` (Art. X — accountability).

| Decision | Owner | Inputs | Policies | Evidence | Outputs |
| -------- | ----- | ------ | -------- | -------- | ------- |
| Admissions Approval | Admissions Officer + Academic Leader | Application, assessment, interview, capacity | Admissions policy, scholarship policy | Application record, references | Offer, cohort placement |
| Scholarship Award | Finance + Academic Leader | Family finance, learner merit | Scholarship policy | Application, supporting documents | Award record, adjusted invoice |
| Promotion / Stage Transition | Academic Leader | Assessment history, teacher input | Assessment policy | Assessment records, portfolio | Promotion decision |
| Assessment Review | Academic Leader | Assessment results, teacher notes | Assessment framework | Marked work, moderation notes | Confirmed results, feedback |
| Support Plan | Wellbeing Lead + Teacher | Wellbeing signals, family input | Wellbeing policy | Notes, referrals | Support plan, review dates |
| Risk Escalation | Any staff → Principal | Incident, signal | Safeguarding policy | Incident record | Response plan |
| Recruitment / Hire | HR + Line Manager | Applications, interviews, references | HR policy | Records | Offer, contract |
| Staff Development Decision | Line Manager + HR | Reviews, learner outcomes | Development framework | Reviews | Development plan |
| Parent Meeting Outcome | Teacher / Mentor + Family | Learner data | Communication policy | Meeting notes | Agreed actions |
| Community Partnership Approval | Community Office + Leadership | Proposal, alignment review | Partnership policy | Proposal, references | Signed partnership |
| Policy Revision | Governance | Performance, incidents, feedback | Meta-policy | Evidence bundle | Revised policy |
| Public Communication | Communications + Leadership | Story, statement | Communications policy | Draft, approvals | Published article / statement |
| Facility Booking Conflict | Operations | Bookings, priority | Operations policy | Booking records | Resolution |

## PART 7 — INSTITUTIONAL INTELLIGENCE OPPORTUNITIES

Per Art. VII, intelligence is the capacity to *See, Understand, Decide,
Coordinate, Respond, Learn — in time*. The following are opportunity
areas — **not** AI products — where ZANOVA will make institutional
intelligence visible.

- **Admissions Intelligence** — Pipeline health, fit signals, cohort composition, conversion timing.
- **Student Success Intelligence** — Multi-signal view of each learner's growth across academic, wellbeing and contribution axes.
- **Attendance Intelligence** — Patterns and early warning without surveillance framing.
- **Assessment Intelligence** — Moderation health, calibration across teachers, outcome trends.
- **Teacher Development Intelligence** — Practice signals aligned to the teaching framework; supports growth, never surveillance.
- **Wellbeing Intelligence** — Latent risk signals, care coverage, escalation timeliness.
- **Community Intelligence** — Partnership alignment, alumni network health, community programme outcomes.
- **Executive Intelligence** — Head of Campus dashboard: execution state across all domains.
- **Institutional Performance Intelligence** — Whole-institution health against the Constitution's principles: knowledge → decision → execution → impact.
- **Operational Intelligence** — Facility utilisation, transport reliability, incident trends.
- **Financial Intelligence** — Enrolment vs. capacity, fee collection health, scholarship distribution.
- **Editorial Intelligence** — Content coverage, story pipeline, publication cadence.

## PART 9 — ZANOVA FOUNDATION — CONSOLIDATION

This section is the contractual index. ZANOVA implementation begins here.

**Domains** — 25 institutional domains (Part 1). Every ZANOVA module
must attach to at least one domain.

**Capabilities** — 17 capability clusters (Part 2). Each carries a
*purpose · owner · digital support · ZANOVA module* contract that
ZANOVA will render as an execution surface.

**Actors** — 17 actor types (Part 3). Each has a defined permissions
envelope and information contract. All actors will federate through
ZANOVA identity.

**Journeys** — 9 canonical institutional journeys (Part 4). Each will
be represented in ZANOVA as an orchestrated flow with visible execution
state (Art. VI).

**Information Objects** — ~60 first-class entities (Part 5). These are
the ZANOVA data schema seed. Every object carries `created_by`,
`updated_by`, `updated_at`, soft-delete and audit trail by default.

**Decisions** — 13 canonical institutional decisions (Part 6). Every
decision produces an immutable `DecisionRecord` linked to its evidence,
policy and outputs.

**Intelligence Opportunities** — 12 opportunity areas (Part 7). ZANOVA
will render each as a signal-driven surface — never as a black-box
score.

**Digital Services** — 18 services (Part 8). Each is registered with
lifecycle state and capability coverage. The public website surfaces
this registry honestly; ZANOVA activates services one by one.
